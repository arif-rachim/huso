1. Cell Theory

The Cell Theory consists of three fundamental principles:

  1. All living things are made of one or more cells - From the simplest bacteria to complex organisms like humans
  2. The cell is the basic unit of life - Cells are the smallest functional unit that can carry out all life processes
  3. All cells come from existing cells - New cells are produced by cell division, not spontaneous generation

üí° Key Insight: This theory revolutionized biology by establishing cells as the foundation of all life.

2. Prokaryotic vs Eukaryotic Cells

Feature Prokaryotic Eukaryotic
Nucleus No membrane-bound nucleus Membrane-bound nucleus
Organelles Few membrane-bound organelles Many specialized organelles
Examples Bacteria, Archaea Plants, Animals, Fungi, Protists

3. Key Cellular Organelles & Functions

üî¨ Universal Organelles (Found in all cells):

  • Cell Membrane: Controls what enters and leaves the cell (selective permeability)
  • Cytoplasm: Jelly-like substance where cellular processes occur
  • Ribosomes: Protein synthesis factories
  • Genetic Material: Contains DNA with instructions for life

üå± Plant-Specific Organelles:

  • Cell Wall: Rigid structure providing support and protection
  • Chloroplasts: Contain chlorophyll for photosynthesis
  • Large Central Vacuole: Maintains turgor pressure and stores water

üêæ Animal Cell Organelles:

  • Nucleus: Control center containing DNA
  • Mitochondria: "Powerhouse" - produces ATP energy
  • Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER): Transport system for materials
  • Golgi Apparatus: "Post office" - packages and ships proteins
  • Lysosomes: "Cleanup crew" - digests waste materials

üî¨ Worked Examples (10-15 min)

Example 1: Identifying Cell Types

Question: A scientist observes a cell under a microscope and notes it has a cell wall, chloroplasts, and a large central vacuole. What type of cell is this?

Solution Steps:

  1. Identify key features: Cell wall, chloroplasts, large central vacuole
  2. Match to cell types: These are plant-specific organelles
  3. Determine cell type: This is a plant cell (eukaryotic)

Answer: Plant cell - The presence of cell wall, chloroplasts, and large central vacuole are distinctive features of plant cells.

Example 2: Organelle Function

Question: Why are mitochondria called the "powerhouses" of the cell?

Solution:

Mitochondria are called powerhouses because:

  • They produce ATP (adenosine triphosphate) through cellular respiration
  • ATP is the cell's main energy currency
  • Without mitochondria, cells couldn't generate the energy needed for life processes
  • They convert glucose and oxygen into usable energy for the cell

Example 3: Cell Theory Application

Question: How does the Cell Theory explain why we don't see spontaneous generation of life?

Solution:

The third principle of Cell Theory states "All cells come from existing cells." This means:

  • New cells only arise through division of pre-existing cells
  • Life cannot spontaneously appear from non-living matter
  • This principle was proven by scientists like Louis Pasteur
  • It explains why sterilized environments remain sterile unless contaminated
